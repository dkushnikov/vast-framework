# VAST in 5 minutes

## The problem

You have a Vision. You have Strategy. You have OKRs. But something is missing — strategies feel disconnected, OKRs degenerate into task lists, teams optimize locally without seeing the whole system.

The missing step: **Architecture** — an explicit understanding of what your system consists of, how domains interact, and what constraints govern decisions.

## The insight

Standard frameworks jump from "why" (Vision/Mission) to "how" (Strategy/OKRs):

```
Standard:   Vision → Strategy → OKRs → Tasks
                  ↑
            Where's the structure?
```

VAST adds the missing waypoint:

```
VAST:       Vision → Architecture → Strategy → Tactics
                         ↑
              "What is our system made of?
               How do domains interact?
               What constraints govern decisions?"
```

## The four layers

| Layer | What it answers | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Vision** | Why do we exist? Where are we going? | "Enable creators to build their own AI" |
| **Architecture** | What domains matter? What constraints hold? | 8 org capabilities + 5 values as structural constraints |
| **Strategy** | Where do we invest? What's priority? | Focus areas + goals for this quarter |
| **Tactics** | What do we build? What do we do today? | Projects, sprints, daily actions |

## The triad

Three layers below Vision map to a clean decomposition:

- **Architecture = Outcomes** — what are we growing?
- **Strategy = Use Cases** — where and how are we working?
- **Tactics = Outputs** — what are we producing?

This is sharper than OKRs, which compress all three into one construct.

## One example

A team notices three data pipelines are broken. They fix them one by one (Tactics). A month later, a fourth breaks.

With VAST: fixing three pipelines is Tactics. "No unified data protocol" is an Architecture gap. Naming the level changes what you build — instead of three fixes, you build the protocol that prevents the fourth.

## What VAST is not

- **Not a replacement** for OKRs, Agile, or your current process. It's an extension — adds Architecture as an explicit waypoint when systems get complex enough to need it.
- **Not universal.** Works above a complexity threshold. If your team fits in one room and everyone knows everything — you don't need VAST.
- **Not new.** Balanced Scorecard, Wardley Maps, Beer's VSM all do Architecture. VAST names it as a step in the canonical sequence. Codification, not invention.
