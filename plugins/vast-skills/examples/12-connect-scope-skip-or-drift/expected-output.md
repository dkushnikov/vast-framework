## 🚨 Fail
- Scope step-down: the "child" is at the **same scope as the parent**, not one level down. Both are company-scope docs — parent: "Northwind exists to give the small operator …" with a capability portfolio of product lines; child: "We will become the system of record for enterprise IT departments" with its own company-level capability portfolio (Access Governance, Audit, Procurement) and CEO-owned Vision. There is no company→product step; this is company↔company. Fix: if Northstar is meant to be a product line *inside* Northwind, re-pitch it at product scope (a composition framework nested under Northwind's portfolio); if it is its own company, it is not a child of this parent at all.
- Vision lineage: the child Vision **contradicts** the parent Vision rather than refining it. Parent: *"give the small operator the leverage of a back office … We win by being the operator's default, not the enterprise's"* — for-whom: *"independent operators and sub-ten-person teams."* Child: *"become the system of record for enterprise IT departments … We win the enterprise: the larger and more regulated the buyer, the better the fit"* — for-whom: *"enterprise IT administrators … at companies of 1,000+ employees."* The child opposes the parent's for-whom and its explicit "not the enterprise" stance — the parent even names enterprise drift as a falsification trigger. This serves no parent Strategy use-case and advances no parent Architecture domain. Fix: either re-anchor the child to an actual parent use-case (the small-operator segment), or recognize it is a different company's Vision, not a child of Northwind's.
- Invariant inheritance: the child does not operate within the parent's invariants — it declares a **competing** set, and one directly contradicts a parent invariant. Parent (Architecture invariant): *"all customer data stays in the customer's home region … no product line replicates, caches, or processes customer data outside the region."* Child (Architecture invariant): *"Multi-region data placement is chosen per enterprise contract (data-residency is a negotiated SLA, not a fixed home region)."* The child reframes an absolute parent guarantee as a negotiable SLA — a contradiction, not an operating-within. (Consistent with the verdict above: this is not a real child inheriting the parent's framework; it is a separate company-scope framework.) Fix: a genuine child inherits the parent's data-residency invariant unchanged; resolve by establishing the true parent or treating this as a non-nested doc.
- Strategy alignment: the child invests **against** the parent's direction. Parent Strategy: invest in operator-facing Inbox → Calendar → Docs, "every product line must earn its place on the operator's daily path." Child Strategy: invest in enterprise Access Governance → Audit → Procurement, "land the largest, most regulated IT departments first." The child's investment pulls toward the exact buyer the parent's Vision excludes (and flags as a falsification trigger). Fix: a child of Northwind sequences toward the small-operator segment; investing in enterprise IT governance is moving away from, not advancing, the parent's priorities.

## Summary
Not VAST-connected — these are two company-scope docs, not a parent→child nesting. The "child" sits at the same scope as the parent (scope step-down fails), its Vision targets enterprise IT in direct opposition to the parent's small-operator stance (Vision lineage fails), it declares a competing invariant set that contradicts the parent's data-residency guarantee (invariant inheritance fails), and its Strategy invests toward the buyer the parent explicitly excludes (Strategy alignment fails). Top fix: decide whether Northstar is a separate company (then it is not a child of Northwind) or a product line within it (then re-pitch at product scope, descend from a Northwind use-case, and inherit Northwind's invariants).
