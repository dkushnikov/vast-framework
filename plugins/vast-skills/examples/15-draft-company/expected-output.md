## Vision
**Answer:** The seed states the ambition, lifted here verbatim-in-spirit: *over three years, become the platform every independent creator uses to build and run their own AI-powered business.* That is a market-stance ambition (the for-whom — independent creators — is present; the enabled experience — build and run an AI-powered business — is present). What it does **not** yet state is the **why** (why this company, why creators, why now), and it has **no falsification triggers** — both required below before this is a committed VAST Vision rather than a mission slogan.
**Guiding questions:**
- Why *this* company for *this* — what's the unique stance or insight that makes "every independent creator" yours to win, versus a horizontal AI platform or an incumbent creator tool? A company Vision is a market stance, not just an aspiration.
- "Every independent creator" is very broad — is there a beachhead (a creator type, a business model) you become the default for *first*, on the way to all? Naming it sharpens the 3-year bet.
- What does "build and run their own AI-powered business" concretely mean for a creator — what can they do on the platform that they can't stitch together themselves today?
**What good looks like:** a committed, falsifiable market-stance hypothesis — "we become {the X for Y} because {stance/insight}" — bounded enough that you'd *know* in three years whether it held. Watch the company-scope failure mode hardest here: do **not** let this Vision collapse into a product feature list or a roadmap (anti-pattern #1, Vision-as-Use-Case, is most acute at company scope — "become the platform" must not quietly become "ship features A, B, C").
**Falsification triggers (required — the most important field on this scaffold):** {to fill — a 3-year company direction is exactly the case where Vision-as-committed-hypothesis lives or dies on its triggers, and exactly the case where seeds omit them. Name 2-5 conditions under which you'd revisit this direction, each **observable** (a real metric — creator activation, retention, share of a creator's business run on-platform, category share), **bounded** (a threshold + duration — note a 3-year Vision wants *leading-indicator* triggers that fire well before year three, not a single year-three verdict), and **owned** (a named leader who monitors it). Per the Vision Falsification Protocol, each trigger also needs a path: who reports to whom, on what cadence, when a review convenes. Do not invent the thresholds — prompt the leadership team for them. Shape, not a value: "{leading creator-adoption metric} fails to reach {threshold} by {date} — owner: {leader}".}

## Architecture
**Answer:** {to fill} — the seed names no org structure or capabilities. Don't invent an org chart; the questions below are the work.
**Guiding questions (company → org / capability-portfolio design — at company scope "Architecture" is the org-as-system, not a literal skill library):**
- **Org capabilities** — what capabilities must the company hold to deliver this Vision (a creator-facing product org? an AI-substrate/platform group? a creator-success/GTM motion? a payments/marketplace capability)? What's the capability portfolio?
- **Team structure** — what teams exist and how do they compose to deliver value (stream-aligned creator-product teams, a platform team owning shared AI capability, enabling teams)? How do they interact?
- **Org-portable invariants (must always hold through any re-org)** — what principles don't change even as titles do? (Every decision has a named owner; span of control bounded; decisions made at the lowest competent layer; values that constrain how domains interact.) Name the ones that are load-bearing for *this* company.
- **Implementations** — the current org chart, reporting lines, RACI/OKR cascade — the parts you expect to refactor as you grow.
- **Who is accountable** for the architecture of the company-as-system (CEO + leadership team)?
**What good looks like:** at company scope this is mostly *shared language for org design*, not a formal composition framework — name the capability portfolio, the team archetypes, and the org-portable invariants, with the leadership team accountable. Be honest that the four-layer shape is functioning here as **vocabulary for company-wide intentionality** (see depth) — don't over-engineer a product-style invariant set (forcing deep where light fits, anti-pattern #6); equally, don't leave Architecture empty and jump to bets (architecture-by-default, anti-pattern #2).
