# VAST for People Leaders

## What this means for People

VAST's most distinctive claim for People: **Values are Architecture, not aspiration.**

Standard frameworks put Values next to Vision — inspirational posters. VAST anchors Values in Architecture — structural constraints that govern how domains interact and how decisions are made.

"Care Intensely" isn't a wish. It's a constraint on how you cut costs (not by degrading quality), how you give feedback (with depth, not surface), how you hire (quality bar over speed).

## What changes

**Values get a structural job.** Instead of "our values are X" → "our values constrain decisions in these specific ways." People function becomes the steward of architectural constraints, not just culture programs.

**People Architecture becomes explicit.** What capabilities exist in the People function (sourcing, assessment, development, performance management), how they compose, what invariants govern them (bias controls, compliance, fairness), what implementations can change (specific tools, specific processes).

**Coaching and development connect to framework.** Self-awareness = prerequisite for understanding Architecture. Coaching programs (like Bravely) become infrastructure investments, not perks.

## What doesn't change

- People still owns culture, engagement, development
- Hiring processes don't need to change overnight
- Existing People programs continue
