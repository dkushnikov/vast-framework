# VAST for CX Leaders

## What this means for CX

CX is where VAST's Architecture concept maps most directly to daily work. You already think in skills (routing, triage, response drafting), compositions (customer journey through those skills), and invariants (response quality, escalation SLAs). VAST just names this structure.

## What changes

**Your capability framework gets explicit.** Instead of ad-hoc AI integrations, you have a named Architecture: what AI skills exist (classifier, router, drafter, summarizer), how they compose (customer journey flows), what invariants hold (accuracy, escalation timing, trust contracts).

**AI integration becomes architectural, not feature-by-feature.** Instead of "add AI to ticket handling" → "what is our CX composition framework, and where does AI fit?" This prevents bolted-on AI that doesn't compose with the rest of the system.

## Worked example

See [applications.md → CX section](../applications.md) for a full VAST application: Vision ("resolve support questions within 1 hour median, AI handling 70%+ tier-1"), Architecture (skill library + invariants), Strategy (quarterly sequencing), Tactics (daily metrics).

## What doesn't change

- CX still owns customer experience end-to-end
- Your team processes and tools don't need to change
- No new meetings or governance overhead

## What you'd want to see

We're applying VAST ourselves. If it works — the proof will be in reduced rework and clearer AI integration decisions. If it doesn't — we'll learn why and share that too.
